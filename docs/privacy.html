Privacy policy

Local Ed AI by DittoEd · Last updated July 2026

The short version: the application collects nothing, transmits nothing, and has no server to send anything to. There is no account, no login, no telemetry, and no analytics. Everything you type stays on the machine you typed it on.

Who this covers

This policy covers the Local Ed AI desktop application and this website. The project is maintained by an individual developer, not a company. There is no data processor, no sub-processor, and no third party with access to anything you enter, because nothing you enter ever leaves your device.

What the application collects

Nothing. There is no collection mechanism in the software.

CategoryCollected?
Personal information (name, email, school)No. There is no account system.
Student data, including IEP contentNo. Processed locally, stored locally, never transmitted.
Chat content and promptsNo. Sent only to the local model on your own machine.
Uploaded or attached filesNo. Stored in local browser storage on your device.
Usage analytics, telemetry, crash reportsNo. None are implemented.
Advertising or tracking identifiersNo. The project has no advertising of any kind.
CookiesNo.

Where your data is stored

On your machine only. Chats, IEP drafts, projects, and saved files are written to IndexedDB, the local storage layer of the application's embedded browser view, inside your own user profile. Nothing is synced, backed up to a server, or replicated to another device. If you delete the application data or the user profile, that content is gone — there is no copy anywhere else, including with the maintainer.

Network connections the application makes

The application makes exactly two categories of outbound request, both of which are visible in the source code:

  • First-run setup (optional, one time). If you do not already have Ollama installed, the app offers to download Ollama and a language model of your choosing. These are downloads of publicly available software; nothing about you or your work is sent as part of them.
  • Local model requests (ongoing). All prompts and generated text travel between the app and Ollama over localhost on your own machine. This traffic never touches a network.

There is no cloud fallback. If your local model is unavailable, the application reports an error rather than reaching out to a hosted service.

This website

This site is static, hosted on GitHub Pages, and sets no cookies and runs no analytics. It makes one client-side request to GitHub's public releases API so the download button can point at the current build. That request goes to GitHub, not to us, and is subject to GitHub's privacy statement. Fonts are loaded from Google Fonts, which means your browser contacts Google's servers to retrieve them.

FERPA, IDEA, and COPPA

Because no student data is collected, transmitted, or stored by any party other than the user's own device, the maintainer is not a "school official" with access to education records, and there is no vendor data flow to govern. Districts evaluating this tool generally find there is nothing to sign a data privacy agreement about, since there is no data sharing to agree on. Your district should still conduct its own review — see the terms and disclaimer.

The current release is a tool for educators, not for direct student use. A separate student-facing build is planned and will carry its own guardrails and its own policy.

Your rights and choices

Rights of access, correction, deletion, and portability are typically exercised by asking a company for a copy of the data it holds about you. There is no such request to make here, because the maintainer holds nothing. You already have complete access to your data, and deleting it is entirely within your control.

Verifying any of this

You do not have to take our word for it. The source is public under Apache 2.0, so you or your IT department can read every network call the application makes, monitor its traffic, or build your own installer from source.

Changes to this policy

This page may be updated as the project evolves. Material changes will be noted here with an updated date. If the project ever begins collecting data of any kind — which is not planned and would contradict its purpose — that change would be announced prominently rather than quietly edited in.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be raised as an issue on the project repository.
